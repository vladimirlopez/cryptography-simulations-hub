Frequency Analysis

The Art of Codebreaking

The Weakness of Substitution

Simple substitution ciphers (like the Caesar Shift) preserve the patterns of the original language. In English, the letter E is the most common, followed by T, A, H, I, N, and S.

If you have a long enough piece of encrypted text, you can count the frequency of each letter. The most frequent character in the ciphertext is likely replacing 'E' in the plaintext.

Patterns Beyond Single Letters

Codebreakers also look for:

  • Bigrams: Pairs of letters like 'TH', 'HE', 'IN', 'ER'.
  • Double Letters: 'LL', 'SS', 'EE', 'OO'.
  • Short Words: 'THE', 'AND', 'OF', 'TO'.

Al-Kindi

The technique was first described by the 9th-century Arab mathematician Al-Kindi. His work marked the beginning of true cryptanalysis, showing that simple substitution ciphers were no longer secure.
