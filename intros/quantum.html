Quantum Cryptography

Using the laws of physics to create unbreakable security.

The Challenge of Key Distribution

In classical cryptography (like Vigenère or One-Time Pad), the biggest problem is: How do Alice and Bob agree on a secret key without Eve intercepting it?

If they send the key over a standard channel, Eve can copy it without anyone knowing.

Enter Quantum Mechanics

Quantum Cryptography, specifically the BB84 Protocol, uses the properties of photons (light particles) to solve this.

According to the No-Cloning Theorem and the observer effect in quantum mechanics, it is impossible to measure a quantum state without disturbing it. If Eve tries to listen in, she introduces errors that Alice and Bob can detect.

How BB84 Works

  1. Alice sends photons polarized in random bases (Rectilinear ✛ or Diagonal ✕).
  2. Bob measures them using random bases.
  3. They publicly compare their bases (not the results).
  4. Where the bases match, they keep the bits. This becomes the shared key.
