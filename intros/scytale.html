The Spartan Scytale

An ancient transposition cipher used by the Spartan military.

History

The scytale (rhymes with "Italy") is one of the earliest known cryptographic devices, used by the Spartans around 400 BC. Unlike the Caesar cipher which changes the identity of letters, the Scytale changes their position. This is known as a transposition cipher.

The Mechanism

The system consisted of a tapered cylinder (the scytale) and a strip of parchment. To encrypt a message, the sender would wrap the parchment spirally around the rod and write the message lengthwise along the rod.

When unwrapped, the strip would appear to contain a random scramble of letters. The only way to decrypt the message was to wrap the strip around a rod of the exact same diameter.

Why it Worked

Without the correct rod diameter, the letters wouldn't align to form words. If you wrapped it around a stick that was too thick or too thin, the letters would meet their neighbors incorrectly, keeping the message hidden.
